How to Help

Signs

First, see if there are signs that someone has mental health problems. Some signs are:

  • Eating or sleeping too much or too little
  • Feeling numb or feel like nothing matters
  • Smoking, drinking, or using drugs more than usual, and thinking of harming themselves

    • Here are more signs that someone could have mental health problems.

    Support

    If they are showing signs and you want to help them or they reached out to you, here are ways to give support:

  • Show that you are concerned and that you are there for them
  • Find out if they are getting the care they need. If not, help them connect to help
  • Reassure them that you care about them
  • If they are feeling suicidal, encourage them to get help. Let them know that you are there.

    • Here are more ways to support someone you care about.

    Hotlines

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 1-800-273-8255 (Available 24/7)
  • The National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI): 1-800-950-6264 (Available Monday–Friday from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. EST)
  • Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA): (800) 662-4357. SAMHSA (Available 24 hours)
  • National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH): (866) 615-6464 (Available Monday–Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. EST)
  • Mental Health America Hotline: Text MHA to 741741
  • The Samaritans: 1-212-673-3000 (Available 24 hours)
  • Veterans Crisis Line: 1-800-273-8255. Text a message to 838255
